Snow Cover Change in Yosemite National Park Over 23 Years (ongoing project)

Background

I spent each summer from ages 5-17 of my life exploring Yosemite Valley; wading through the Merced River, hiking Vernal and Nevada Falls, and biking up to Mirror Lake. During those years, I began to take note of fluctuations in the Merced River’s water depth and the flow rate of Yosemite Falls, one of Yosemite’s most notable waterfalls. These observations inspired me to take higher ground, investigating how snow cover has shifted in Yosemite’s watershed.

In this ongoing project, I focus on monitoring snow cover changes in Yosemite National Park to investigate the implications to water resources. This project examines the temporal changes in winter snow cover in Yosemite National Park from 2000 to 2023. Utilizing Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) satellite data, I aim to identify trends of snow cover change and to contribute to our understanding of climatic impacts on Yosemite’s snowpacks and regional hydrology.

Analyses

Using earth observation data from MODIS, I:

  • Calculated annual winter (Dec-Mar) snow cover area across Yosemite, creating a time lapse with the resultant GEOTIFFs.
  • Performed a time serires analysis to track changes snow area cover change.
  • Conducted a change detection analysis between the first and last year in the study period.
