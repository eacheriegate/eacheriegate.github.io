PORTFOLIO ABOUT RESUME

Modeling Multi-Decadal Trajectories of Ghost Forest Development and Environmental Drivers

Background

This project builds upon the foundational work completed during my summer tenure with NASA in 2023. It is currently under development for a forthcoming manuscript, with details to be released upon the publication's DOI assignment.

In this study we are:

  • Performing a multiple time series analysis of:
    • Random forest land cover classification and vegetative health indicators (NDVI/EVI) to assess ghost forest (GF) extent.
    • Key environmental drivers including sea level rise (SLR), saltwater intrusion (SWI), groundwater levels, and storm surges.
  • Conducting a multivariate time series analysis (vector autoregression, VAR) to examine the relationships between these factors.

The goal is to uncover potential seasonal (growing vs dormant) relationships between GFs and these drivers from 1990 to 2023 in Savannah, Georgia. This research seeks to improve the application of remote sensing for monitoring SWI and its coastal vegetative impacts within the context of SLR and climate change.
