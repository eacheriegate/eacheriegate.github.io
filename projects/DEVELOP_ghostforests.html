PORTFOLIO ABOUT RESUME

Ghost Forest Development along the Savannah River

Background

While participating in NASA-JPL’s DEVELOP program, I led a team that addressed the impact of saltwater intrusion (SWI) and sea level rise (SLR) on coastal ecosystems. This influx of saltwater inland has led to the emergence of "ghost forests"—areas where freshwater forested wetlands have turned into marshland due to saltwater influxes. The Savannah River in Georgia, with its low-lying geography, is particularly vulnerable to these changes.

This project aimed to analyze the development of ghost forests due to SWI along the Savannah River in Georgia using earth observation data, from 2013 to 2023.

Analyses

Utilizing satellite imagery from Landsat 7 ETM+ & 8 OLI and Planet’s RapidEye and Dove sensors, we:

  • Calculated NDVI time series to assess vegetation health and sensor performance.
  • Performed random forest land cover classification time series to track changes in class types.
  • Conducted in situ porewater salinity time series to establish a correlation between salinity trends and vegetative changes.
