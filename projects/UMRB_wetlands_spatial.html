Spatial Relationships of Historical Wetlands

Background

In the agricultural Upper Mississippi Basin (UMRB), nitrogen (N) pollution from excess fertilizer presents an issue for terrestrial and aquatic life. It can contribute to higher traces of nitrate in local drinking water supplies, depleted oxygen levels posing harm to aquatic biodiversity, and harmful algal blooms. In this collaborative NASA and USDA-funded project, my team looked to wetlands as a nature-based solution due to their potential to reduce the excess nitrate.

We mapped over 4 million historical (drained) wetlands in the UMRB using a remote sensing and GIS-based methodology that integrated topographic (digital elevation models), hydrologic, soil, and land use/land cover data. By developing this dataset, we aimed to support policymakers and watershed managers in identifying key areas for wetland restoration and aid modeling efforts to quantify the water quality benefits associated with such restoration.
